Design and Fabrication of a Three-Flyball Centrifugal Governer

The primary objective of completing this project was to learn the design process, which is not as simple as only creating a 3D model of a product, but analyzing the need for the product and what steps need to be taken to produce it from scratch. I wondered why I had only seen centrifugal governors with two flyballs in all my engineering labs, and that was my motivation to pursue this project and determine how the weight of the flyballs affect the stresses, strains and the life of the product. The two flyball version designed entails two 5 Kg flyballs whereas the three flyball version entails both 3 flyballs with three 5 Kg flyballs and and three flyballs with three 3.3 Kg flyballs. The result concludes that the three flyball with 5 Kg flyballs each would turn out to be the optimum choice in case for longevity and structural performance.

The steps to create this design were:

1. Listing out the components.
2. Select material for each component using the weighted point system.
3. Calculate the required dimensions like height, diameter etc. of various components using stress analysis.
4. Create individual parts and assemble.
5. Perform mechanism simulation and Stress analysis.

This project and the simulations were also used to as teaching material for the course Mechanics of Machines (UME 306)
