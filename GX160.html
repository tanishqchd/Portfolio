Reverse Engineering and Designing of Honda GX160 I.C. Engine

This project was a part of my course on Computer Aided Design and Analysis, wherein we took apart a Honda GX160 I.C. Engine, broke it down to all its components, measured more than 30 of them, 3D-modelled them in PTC Creo and made a few adjustments to the designs.

A major component of this project was the balancing of the Center of Gravity of the crankshaft. For doing this, the original measures of the crankshaft were carefully taken and modelled in the software, then using the 'Behavioural Modeller Extension' module in Creo, sensitivity analysis was performed relative to various dimensions, and at last the one with the least final weight was finalized.

Unfortunately, as this project was performed on the University systems, I do not have pictures of the 3D designs.
